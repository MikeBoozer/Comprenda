# Nuance — Demo Script (5 minutes)

Record on Loom. One take, no editing. Casual but confident. Aim for 5 minutes; cut if longer.

## Pre-recording checklist

- [ ] Snowflake Streamlit app open in browser tab
- [ ] All pages pre-loaded (no spinner during recording)
- [ ] Draft tagline ready: `"Live Free, Drive Fast — the personal electric sports car that puts you first."`
- [ ] Target markets to score: `ja, zh, de, es`
- [ ] One specific real-world cultural-failure story ready as the cold-open

---

## 0:00 - 0:30 — The Hook

> "Quick story. In 1990, Mercedes-Benz launched in China under the name 'Bensi.' In Mandarin, that reads phonetically as 'rush to die.' By the time someone noticed, the launch was already off the rails."

> "Every multinational marketing team has a version of that story. And in 2026 it's getting worse — because AI agents are now drafting global content faster than human reviewers can vet it."

> "I built Nuance to fix this. Let me show you."

---

## 0:30 - 1:30 — Pre-Launch Risk Score (the killer feature)

Open the Pre-Launch Risk page.

> "I'm a marketing director. I'm about to ship this tagline — 'Live Free, Drive Fast — the personal electric sports car that puts you first.' Sounds fine in English. But I'm launching in four markets."

Paste the tagline. Pick targets `ja, zh, de, es`. Click **Score cultural risk**.

> "In eight seconds, Nuance scores cultural risk per market — 0 to 100."

When results land:

> "Japan: 68. High risk. The tagline trips an individualist frame, but Japanese product discussion runs heavily collectivist and status-quo. Notice the narrative — it cites three specific historical analogs. Click through, and I can see Mercedes Bensi, Tropicana 2009, and the Walmart Germany exit are the closest semantic neighbors."

> "Germany: 41. Medium. The pragmatism is fine; the personal-freedom angle reads as marketing-speak rather than substance."

> "China: 72. The 'drive fast' frame collides with nationalist-pride dynamics in a market that prefers domestic EV brands. Notice the analogs again — D&G chopsticks 2018, Burger King Texican 2009."

---

## 1:30 - 2:30 — Cultural Translator (the workflow value)

> "OK so we have a high-risk tagline in two markets. What do I do? Most tools end here. Nuance doesn't."

Click the suggestion link to Cultural Translator (or just navigate there). Paste the same tagline, target = `ja`.

Click **Generate adapted variants**.

> "Three variants in Japanese, each shifting the cultural frame deliberately. The first goes collectivist — 'a car the whole family enjoys.' The second goes pragmatic — 'engineered for the way you actually drive.' Third stays close to the original but adds a community angle."

> "These are drop-in usable. My team takes them, picks one, ships. Nuance pays for itself the first time it prevents a launch redo."

---

## 2:30 - 3:30 — Post-launch monitoring

Navigate to Divergence Matrix → pick `BrandX_rebrand`.

> "After the launch, Nuance tracks how content actually performed. This is the Cultural Divergence Score heatmap for a rebrand we analyzed."

> "See the dark red between English and Japanese? That's a CDS of 0.71 — the two communities are reading this rebrand *completely differently*. Brandwatch would have told you both communities had 'mostly negative sentiment' and stopped there. Nuance tells you *why* it's different — and we can drill down into the cultural frames."

Navigate to Frame Distribution.

> "In English, the rebrand was framed as an opportunity. In Japanese, threat-framing and status-quo dominate. That's actionable. That's a different press release, a different campaign, a different recovery strategy."

---

## 3:30 - 4:30 — AI Brief + Drift Alerts

Navigate to AI Brief Generator → same event. Click **Generate brief**.

> "And when the CMO asks 'what's happening across our markets,' Nuance generates a 2-page brief. Cited, structured, ready to drop in a board deck."

(Show the brief rendering.)

Navigate to Drift Alerts.

> "And this all becomes always-on. Subscribe your brand. If cultural divergence spikes — say, between Korean and English speakers around a campaign — you get an email or Slack ping before it shows up in the trade press."
