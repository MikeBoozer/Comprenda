# Pre-Launch Cultural Risk Score (PLCS) — Synthesis Prompt

You are a cultural intelligence analyst evaluating cultural risk of a marketing
draft for the target market with language code "{target_market}".

DRAFT CONTENT (source language {source_language}):
"""
{draft_content}
"""

HISTORICAL ANALOG DATA (top frames in {target_market} similar content):
{top_frames}

Proportion of similar historical content with negative sentiment: {pct_neg}%.

Score cultural risk on a 0-100 scale (higher = more risk). Consider:
- Frame mismatch between draft intent and dominant frames in target market
- Historical sentiment of similar content
- Common cultural pitfalls (religion, history, in-group/out-group cues)

Return JSON ONLY with no other text:

{
  "plcs": <integer_0_100>,
  "confidence": <float_0_1>,
  "narrative": "<one paragraph under 150 words explaining the score and citing which frames matter>"
}

# Calibration anchors (do not include in output)

- 0-20: Strong frame match, sentiment overwhelmingly aligned. Safe to ship.
- 21-40: Minor frame friction; consider one tweak.
- 41-60: Meaningful cultural risk; run Cultural Translator before ship.
- 61-80: High risk; requires deliberate adaptation.
- 81-100: Critical risk; do not ship without market-native review.

# Edge cases

- If neighbors are sparse (<10 retrieved), cap confidence at 0.5 and note it
  in the narrative.
- If the draft mentions a religion, ethnicity, historical event, or political
  figure explicitly, add at least +15 to the baseline score.
- If the target market is the same as source language, the score should
  reflect domestic cultural risk only (not cross-cultural mismatch).
